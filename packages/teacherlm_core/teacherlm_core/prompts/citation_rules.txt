Citation Rules
==============

FORMAT
------
- Cite inline, immediately after the claim it supports: "(from {filename})".
- If multiple files support one claim, list them comma-separated: "(from syllabus.pdf, week3_notes.md)".
- If a page or section is provided in the chunk metadata, include it: "(from textbook.pdf, p. 42)" or "(from lecture.md, §2.1)".
- Use the filename exactly as it appears in the chunk's `source` field — don't prettify, translate, or abbreviate it.

WHEN TO CITE
------------
- Cite every factual claim, definition, formula, date, name, or quoted phrase drawn from the uploaded files.
- Cite at least once per paragraph that leans on the materials, even if it feels repetitive.
- Grouped list items may share one trailing citation if they all come from the same source: "... (from week3_notes.md)".
- General study guidance or meta-comments about the conversation itself do not need citations.

WHEN YOU CANNOT CITE
--------------------
- If the answer is not supported by any chunk in context, do not invent a citation.
- Instead, say plainly that the uploaded materials don't cover it, and offer the nearest related topic that IS covered.
- Never cite external knowledge, Wikipedia, or the model's own training data.

QUOTING
-------
- Short direct quotes (under ~20 words) go in double quotes followed by the citation: "\"energy is conserved in a closed system\" (from physics_ch2.pdf)".
- Longer passages should be paraphrased, then cited — don't dump long verbatim blocks.

CONSISTENCY
-----------
- One citation style per response. Don't mix "(from X)" with "[X]" or footnote numbers.
- Citations go at the END of the sentence or clause, before the period: "Photosynthesis requires light (from bio_ch4.pdf)."
