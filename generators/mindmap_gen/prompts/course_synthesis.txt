You are merging extracted notes from all parts of uploaded course materials
into ONE NotebookLM-style study mind map.

The input contains several partial outlines extracted from different batches.
They may overlap, repeat, or use lecture/week/file titles. Your job is to
merge them into a clean conceptual hierarchy.

Target shape:
- Central topic: the real course subject, not a file name, week number, broad
  school subject, or one lecture title.
- {n_branches} major branches: the main modules students should study.
- Each major branch: 2-5 subtopics.
- Each subtopic: 0-4 concrete leaf details.

Quality rules:
- Use ONLY information that appears in the extracted outlines.
- Keep the same language as the source materials.
- Cover the whole course, not just the first or most recent lecture.
- Preserve coverage across all modules. Do not let one long module, guide, or
  supplemental reference dominate the final map.
- Merge duplicates and near-duplicates.
- Convert lecture/week/file titles into conceptual modules when possible.
- Keep siblings at the same abstraction level.
- Put specific facts under the correct broader idea: definitions, causes,
  consequences, dates, people, places, formulas, methods, classifications,
  examples, limitations, metrics, debates, and case studies.
- Do not force any subject template. For history, chronology may be best; for
  geography, regions/processes may be best; for math, definitions/methods/
  formulas may be best; for computer science, concepts/algorithms/evaluation
  may be best; for medicine, anatomy/pathology/treatment may be best; for law,
  doctrines/cases/arguments may be best; for literature, themes/characters/
  movements may be best.
- Avoid generic branches such as "Introduction", "Conclusion", "Concepts",
  "Applications", "Other", "Python", "Mathematics", or "Computer Science"
  unless the extracted course truly uses one as a major section.
- Prefer concise study labels over long slide titles.
- If partial outlines come from multiple documents, choose main branches that
  cover the whole course progression rather than copying a single document's
  title as the root.

Return ONLY the structured hierarchy.
